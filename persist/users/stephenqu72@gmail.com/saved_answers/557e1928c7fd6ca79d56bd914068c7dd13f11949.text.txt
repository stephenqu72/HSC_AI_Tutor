### Part i

To find the point of intersection between the line $\underset{\sim}{R}(\lambda) = \underset{\sim}{a} + \lambda\underset{\sim}{b}$ and the sphere $S$ defined by $|\underset{\sim}{v} - \underset{\sim}{c}| = r$, we substitute the expression for the line into the sphere equation:

$$|\underset{\sim}{a} + \lambda\underset{\sim}{b} - \underset{\sim}{c}| = r$$

Squaring both sides to eliminate the square root inherent in the modulus definition $|\underset{\sim}{u}| = \sqrt{\underset{\sim}{u} \cdot \underset{\sim}{u}}$:

$$(\underset{\sim}{a} + \lambda\underset{\sim}{b} - \underset{\sim}{c}) \cdot (\underset{\sim}{a} + \lambda\underset{\sim}{b} - \underset{\sim}{c}) = r^2$$

Rearranging the terms to group the $\lambda$ components:
$$(\lambda\underset{\sim}{b} + (\underset{\sim}{a} - \underset{\sim}{c})) \cdot (\lambda\underset{\sim}{b} + (\underset{\sim}{a} - \underset{\sim}{c})) = r^2$$

Expanding the dot product using the distributive property:
$$\lambda^2(\underset{\sim}{b} \cdot \underset{\sim}{b}) + 2\lambda\underset{\sim}{b} \cdot (\underset{\sim}{a} - \underset{\sim}{c}) + (\underset{\sim}{a} - \underset{\sim}{c}) \cdot (\underset{\sim}{a} - \underset{\sim}{c}) = r^2$$
$$\lambda^2|\underset{\sim}{b}|^2 + 2\lambda\underset{\sim}{b} \cdot (\underset{\sim}{a} - \underset{\sim}{c}) + |\underset{\sim}{a} - \underset{\sim}{c}|^2 - r^2 = 0$$

This is a quadratic equation in $\lambda$ of the form $A\lambda^2 + B\lambda + C = 0$. Since the line is tangent to the sphere, there is exactly one point of intersection, meaning the discriminant $\Delta = B^2 - 4AC$ must be zero. However, we are looking for the value of $\lambda$ at the point of contact. For a quadratic equation with a single root, the root is given by $\lambda = -B / (2A)$.

Substituting $A = |\underset{\sim}{b}|^2$ and $B = 2\underset{\sim}{b} \cdot (\underset{\sim}{a} - \underset{\sim}{c})$:
$$\lambda = -\frac{2\underset{\sim}{b} \cdot (\underset{\sim}{a} - \underset{\sim}{c})}{2|\underset{\sim}{b}|^2} = \frac{\underset{\sim}{b} \cdot (\underset{\sim}{c} - \underset{\sim}{a})}{|\underset{\sim}{b}|^2}$$

Thus, the position vector of the point of contact is $R(\lambda)$ where $\lambda = \frac{\underset{\sim}{b} \cdot (\underset{\sim}{c} - \underset{\sim}{a})}{|\underset{\sim}{b}|^2}$.

### Part ii

To show that the tangent is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact, we must show that the dot product of the direction vector of the line ($\underset{\sim}{b}$) and the radius vector at the point of contact ($\underset{\sim}{R}(\lambda) - \underset{\sim}{c}$) is zero.

From Part (i), the point of contact is $\underset{\sim}{R}(\lambda) = \underset{\sim}{a} + \lambda\underset{\sim}{b}$ with $\lambda = \frac{\underset{\sim}{b} \cdot (\underset{\sim}{c} - \underset{\sim}{a})}{|\underset{\sim}{b}|^2}$.
The radius vector is $\underset{\sim}{v}_{rad} = \underset{\sim}{a} + \lambda\underset{\sim}{b} - \underset{\sim}{c}$. We calculate $\underset{\sim}{b} \cdot \underset{\sim}{v}_{rad}$:

$$\underset{\sim}{b} \cdot (\underset{\sim}{a} + \lambda\underset{\sim}{b} - \underset{\sim}{c}) = \underset{\sim}{b} \cdot (\underset{\sim}{a} - \underset{\sim}{c}) + \lambda(\underset{\sim}{b} \cdot \underset{\sim}{b})$$
$$\underset{\sim}{b} \cdot (\underset{\sim}{a} - \underset{\sim}{c}) + \left( \frac{\underset{\sim}{b} \cdot (\underset{\sim}{c} - \underset{\sim}{a})}{|\underset{\sim}{b}|^2} \right) |\underset{\sim}{b}|^2$$
$$= \underset{\sim}{b} \cdot (\underset{\sim}{a} - \underset{\sim}{c}) + \underset{\sim}{b} \cdot (\underset{\sim}{c} - \underset{\sim}{a})$$
$$= \underset{\sim}{b} \cdot (\underset{\sim}{a} - \underset{\sim}{c} + \underset{\sim}{c} - \underset{\sim}{a}) = \underset{\sim}{b} \cdot \underset{\sim}{0} = 0$$

Since the dot product is zero, the tangent line direction vector $\underset{\sim}{b}$ is perpendicular to the radius vector $\underset{\sim}{R}(\lambda) - \underset{\sim}{c}$.
