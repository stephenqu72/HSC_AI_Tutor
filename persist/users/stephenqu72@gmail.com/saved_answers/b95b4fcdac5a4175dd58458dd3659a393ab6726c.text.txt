To solve this problem, we use the properties of an equilateral triangle with side length 1 in the $xy$-plane, and the properties of a regular tetrahedron where all faces are equilateral triangles of side length 1.

### Part i
**Task:** Find the coordinates of $E$.

The base $\triangle ODE$ is an equilateral triangle in the $xy$-plane with vertices $O(0,0,0)$ and $D(1,0,0)$. For an equilateral triangle with side length $s=1$, the $x$-coordinate of the third vertex is the midpoint of the base, $x = \frac{1}{2}$, and the $y$-coordinate is given by the height of the triangle, $h = \sqrt{s^2 - (\frac{s}{2})^2} = \sqrt{1^2 - (\frac{1}{2})^2} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$. Since $E$ is in the $xy$-plane, its $z$-coordinate is $0$.

$$E = \left(\frac{1}{2}, \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}, 0\right)$$

**Final Answer:** $E = \left(\frac{1}{2}, \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}, 0\right)$

### Part ii
**Task:** Find the coordinates of $F$.

Let $F = (x_F, y_F, z_F)$. Since $F$ is a vertex of the tetrahedron connected to $O, D,$ and $E$, all edges have length 1.
1.  **Distance from $O(0,0,0)$:** $x_F^2 + y_F^2 + z_F^2 = 1^2 = 1$.
2.  **Distance from $D(1,0,0)$:** $(x_F-1)^2 + y_F^2 + z_F^2 = 1$.
    Expanding this: $x_F^2 - 2x_F + 1 + y_F^2 + z_F^2 = 1$.
    Substituting $x_F^2 + y_F^2 + z_F^2 = 1$ into this equation: $1 - 2x_F + 1 = 1 \implies 2x_F = 1 \implies x_F = \frac{1}{2}$.
3.  **Distance from $E(\frac{1}{2}, \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}, 0)$:** $(x_F - \frac{1}{2})^2 + (y_F - \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2})^2 + z_F^2 = 1$.
    Substitute $x_F = \frac{1}{2}$: $0^2 + (y_F - \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2})^2 + z_F^2 = 1$.
    $y_F^2 - \sqrt{3}y_F + \frac{3}{4} + z_F^2 = 1$.
    From step 1, $x_F^2 + y_F^2 + z_F^2 = 1 \implies (\frac{1}{2})^2 + y_F^2 + z_F^2 = 1 \implies y_F^2 + z_F^2 = \frac{3}{4}$.
    Substitute $y_F^2 + z_F^2 = \frac{3}{4}$ into the equation from step 3:
    $\frac{3}{4} - \sqrt{3}y_F + \frac{3}{4} = 1 \implies \frac{6}{4} - \sqrt{3}y_F = 1 \implies \sqrt{3}y_F = \frac{1}{2} \implies y_F = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{3}} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{6}$.
4.  **Find $z_F$:** $z_F^2 = \frac{3}{4} - y_F^2 = \frac{3}{4} - (\frac{\sqrt{3}}{6})^2 = \frac{3}{4} - \frac{3}{36} = \frac{27}{36} - \frac{3}{36} = \frac{24}{36} = \frac{2}{3}$.
    Since $F$ is above the $xy$-plane, $z_F = \sqrt{\frac{2}{3}} = \frac{\sqrt{6}}{3}$.

**Final Answer:** $F = \left(\frac{1}{2}, \frac{\sqrt{3}}{6}, \frac{\sqrt{6}}{3}\right)$
