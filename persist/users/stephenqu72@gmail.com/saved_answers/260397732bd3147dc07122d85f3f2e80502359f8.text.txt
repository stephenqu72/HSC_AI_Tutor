To determine the contrapositive of a conditional statement "if $P$, then $Q$", we use the logical rule:
The contrapositive is "if (not $Q$), then (not $P$)".

### Step 1: Identify $P$ and $Q$
The given statement is: "if $x > y > 0$, then $0 < \frac{1}{x} < \frac{1}{y}$".

*   **Hypothesis ($P$):** $x > y > 0$
*   **Conclusion ($Q$):** $0 < \frac{1}{x} < \frac{1}{y}$

### Step 2: Formulate (not $Q$) and (not $P$)
*   **Negation of $Q$ ($\neg Q$):** 
    The original conclusion $Q$ is $(0 < \frac{1}{x})$ AND $(\frac{1}{x} < \frac{1}{y})$.
    By De Morgan’s Laws, the negation of $(A \land B)$ is $(\neg A \lor \neg B)$.
    $\neg (0 < \frac{1}{x})$ is $0 \ge \frac{1}{x}$.
    $\neg (\frac{1}{x} < \frac{1}{y})$ is $\frac{1}{x} \ge \frac{1}{y}$.
    Therefore, $\neg Q$ is: $0 \ge \frac{1}{x}$ or $\frac{1}{x} \ge \frac{1}{y}$.

*   **Negation of $P$ ($\neg P$):**
    The original hypothesis $P$ is $(x > y)$ AND $(y > 0)$.
    By De Morgan’s Laws, the negation is:
    $\neg (x > y)$ OR $\neg (y > 0)$
    Which is: $x \le y$ OR $y \le 0$.

### Step 3: Construct the Contrapositive
Combining these using the structure "if $\neg Q$, then $\neg P$":
"If $(0 \ge \frac{1}{x} \text{ or } \frac{1}{x} \ge \frac{1}{y})$, then $(x \le y \text{ or } y \le 0)$."
