To determine the projection of the curve $r(t) = (t^2, t^4, t^6)$ onto the $xz$-plane, we analyze the relationship between the $x$-coordinate and the $z$-coordinate.

### Step 1: Identify the components
The curve is defined by the parametric equations:
- $x = t^2$
- $y = t^4$
- $z = t^6$

### Step 2: Establish the relationship for the projection
The projection onto the $xz$-plane ignores the $y$-coordinate and considers only the relationship between $x$ and $z$. We are given:
- $x = t^2$
- $z = t^6$

Since $x = t^2$, we can express $z$ in terms of $x$ by substituting $t^2$ into the expression for $z$:
$$z = (t^2)^3$$
$$z = x^3$$

### Step 3: Analyse the domain and behaviour
- **Domain of $x$**: Since $x = t^2$ and $t$ is any real value, $x$ must be greater than or equal to zero ($x \ge 0$).
- **Relationship**: For $x \ge 0$, the relationship is $z = x^3$.
    - When $x = 0$, $z = 0^3 = 0$.
    - When $x = 1$, $z = 1^3 = 1$.
    - When $x = 2$, $z = 2^3 = 8$.
