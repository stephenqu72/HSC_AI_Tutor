$$\vec{e} = \frac{\vec{a} + \vec{b}}{2}$$

$M$ is the midpoint of the line segment $EF$. By the midpoint formula for vectors, the position vector $\vec{m}$ (representing $\vec{OM}$) is the average of the position vectors of $E$ and $F$:
$$\vec{m} = \frac{\vec{e} + \vec{f}}{2}$$

$$\vec{m} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \left( \frac{\vec{a} + \vec{b}}{2} \right) + \left( \frac{\vec{c} + \vec{d}}{2} \right) \right)$$

$$\vec{m} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{\vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c} + \vec{d}}{2} \right)$$

$$\vec{m} = \frac{\vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c} + \vec{d}}{4}$$

$$\vec{OM} = \frac{1}{4}(\vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c} + \vec{d})$$
