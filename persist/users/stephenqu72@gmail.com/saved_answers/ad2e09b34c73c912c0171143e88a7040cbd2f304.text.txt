### Part ii

First, we find the lengths of the two segments using the distance formula. One unit = 500 metres = 0.5 km.

**Segment 1:** From $A(-9, 15, 7)$ to $(5, 5, 5)$.
The vector $\vec{AP} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 - (-9) \\ 5 - 15 \\ 5 - 7 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 14 \\ -10 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}$.
Length $|\vec{AP}| = \sqrt{14^2 + (-10)^2 + (-2)^2} = \sqrt{196 + 100 + 4} = \sqrt{300} = 10\sqrt{3}$ units.

**Segment 2:** From $B(33, 5, -1)$ to $(5, 5, 5)$.
The vector $\vec{BP} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 - 33 \\ 5 - 5 \\ 5 - (-1) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -28 \\ 0 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix}$.
Length $|\vec{BP}| = \sqrt{(-28)^2 + 0^2 + 6^2} = \sqrt{784 + 36} = \sqrt{820} \approx 28.636$ units.

**Total length in units:**
Total $= 10\sqrt{3} + \sqrt{820} \approx 17.321 + 28.636 = 45.957$ units.

**Convert to kilometres:**
Since 1 unit = 0.5 km:
Total distance $= 45.957 \times 0.5 \text{ km} = 22.9785 \text{ km}$.
Rounding to the nearest kilometre, we get 23 km.

**Final Answer:** The total length is 23 km.
