To find the value of $\tan \alpha$, we must analyse the forces acting on the particle of mass $m$ while it is in a state of static equilibrium.

### Worked Solution

**1. Identify the forces acting on the particle:**
The particle is in equilibrium, meaning the vector sum of all forces acting on it must be zero ($\sum \vec{F} = 0$). The three forces acting on the particle are:
*   **Weight ($mg$):** Acting vertically downwards.
*   **Horizontal force ($F$):** Acting horizontally to the right.
*   **Tension ($T$):** Acting along the string. Since the string makes an angle $\alpha$ with the horizontal, we resolve the tension into horizontal and vertical components.

**2. Resolve the tension force ($T$):**
Using the angle $\alpha$ given with the horizontal:
*   The horizontal component of the tension acts to the left: $T_x = T \cos \alpha$.
*   The vertical component of the tension acts upwards: $T_y = T \sin \alpha$.

**3. Apply the conditions for static equilibrium:**
For the system to be in equilibrium, the net force in both the horizontal ($x$) and vertical ($y$) directions must be zero.

*   **Horizontal Equilibrium ($\sum F_x = 0$):**
    Taking right as positive:
    $$F - T \cos \alpha = 0$$
    $$F = T \cos \alpha \quad \dots \text{(Equation 1)}$$

*   **Vertical Equilibrium ($\sum F_y = 0$):**
    Taking up as positive:
    $$T \sin \alpha - mg = 0$$
    $$T \sin \alpha = mg \quad \dots \text{(Equation 2)}$$

**4. Solve for $\tan \alpha$:**
We know that $\tan \alpha = \frac{\sin \alpha}{\cos \alpha}$. To isolate this, we divide Equation 2 by Equation 1:
$$\frac{T \sin \alpha}{T \cos \alpha} = \frac{mg}{F}$$
Cancelling the tension magnitude $T$ (where $T \neq 0$):
$$\tan \alpha = \frac{mg}{F}$$

The value of $\tan \alpha$ is $\frac{mg}{F}$.
