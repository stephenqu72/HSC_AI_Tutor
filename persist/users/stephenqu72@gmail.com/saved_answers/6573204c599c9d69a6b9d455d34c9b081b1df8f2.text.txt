### Part b

We wish to prove that $5^{2n} - 1$ is divisible by 8 for all positive integers $n$.

Note that $5^{2n} = (5^2)^n = 25^n$.
Using the binomial expansion, we can write $25^n = (1 + 24)^n$.
By the binomial theorem:
$$(1 + 24)^n = \binom{n}{0}(1)^n + \binom{n}{1}(1)^{n-1}(24) + \binom{n}{2}(1)^{n-2}(24)^2 + \dots + \binom{n}{n}(24)^n$$
$$25^n = 1 + 24n + \binom{n}{2}(24)^2 + \dots + (24)^n$$
Therefore:
$$5^{2n} - 1 = (1 + 24n + \binom{n}{2}(24)^2 + \dots + 24^n) - 1$$
$$5^{2n} - 1 = 24n + 24^2 \left( \binom{n}{2} + \dots \right)$$
$$5^{2n} - 1 = 8(3n + 72 \times (\text{terms involving powers of 24}))$$
Since 8 is a factor of the expression, $5^{2n} - 1$ is divisible by 8 for all positive integers $n$.

Final Answer: $5^{2n} - 1$ is divisible by 8.
