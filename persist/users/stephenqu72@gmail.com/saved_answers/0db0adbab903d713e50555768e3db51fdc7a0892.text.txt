To find the velocity $v$ in terms of displacement $x$ given the acceleration $a$ as a function of $x$, we use the identity for acceleration:
$$a = \frac{1}{2} \frac{d}{dx}(v^2)$$

### Step 1: Set up the differential equation
Given $a = 2x^3 - 5x$, we substitute this into the identity:
$$\frac{1}{2} \frac{d}{dx}(v^2) = 2x^3 - 5x$$
Multiply by 2 to isolate the derivative:
$$\frac{d}{dx}(v^2) = 4x^3 - 10x$$

### Step 2: Integrate with respect to $x$
Integrate both sides with respect to $x$:
$$v^2 = \int (4x^3 - 10x) \, dx$$
$$v^2 = x^4 - 5x^2 + C$$

### Step 3: Solve for the constant of integration $C$
We are given the initial condition that $v = 2$ when $x = 0$. Substituting these values into our expression for $v^2$:
$$2^2 = (0)^4 - 5(0)^2 + C$$
$$4 = 0 - 0 + C \implies C = 4$$
Thus, the expression for $v^2$ is:
$$v^2 = x^4 - 5x^2 + 4$$
Taking the square root (noting that $v$ can be positive or negative):
$$v = \pm \sqrt{x^4 - 5x^2 + 4}$$

### Step 4: Determine the possible values of $x$
For the velocity $v$ to be a real number, we require $v^2 \geq 0$. We must solve the inequality:
$$x^4 - 5x^2 + 4 \geq 0$$
Let $u = x^2$. The inequality becomes $u^2 - 5u + 4 \geq 0$. Factorising the quadratic:
$$(u - 4)(u - 1) \geq 0$$
The roots are $u = 1$ and $u = 4$. The inequality holds when $u \leq 1$ or $u \geq 4$.
Substituting back $u = x^2$:
1. $x^2 \leq 1 \implies -1 \leq x \leq 1$
2. $x^2 \geq 4 \implies x \leq -2$ or $x \geq 2$

**Final Answer:**
The expression for velocity is $v = \pm \sqrt{x^4 - 5x^2 + 4}$ (or $v = \pm \sqrt{(x^2-1)(x^2-4)}$).
The possible values for $x$ are $x \in (-\infty, -2] \cup [-1, 1] \cup
 [2, \infty)$.
