### Part (a)
To prove $3^n > n^3$ for all integers $n > 3$ by mathematical induction:

**Base Case:** Let $n = 4$.
LHS: $3^4 = 81$.
RHS: $4^3 = 64$.
Since $81 > 64$, the base case holds.

**Inductive Step:** Assume the statement is true for $n = k$, where $k \ge 4$. That is, assume $3^k > k^3$. We must show that $3^{k+1} > (k+1)^3$.

Starting with the assumption $3^k > k^3$:
Multiply both sides by 3:
$3 \cdot 3^k > 3k^3 \implies 3^{k+1} > 3k^3$.
We need to prove $3k^3 > (k+1)^3 = k^3 + 3k^2 + 3k + 1$.
Consider the difference $f(k) = 2k^3 - 3k^2 - 3k - 1$.
For $k=4$, $f(4) = 2(64) - 3(16) - 3(4) - 1 = 128 - 48 - 12 - 1 = 67 > 0$.
Since the derivative $f'(k) = 6k^2 - 6k - 3 > 0$ for $k \ge 4$, $f(k)$ is increasing.
Thus, $3k^3 > (k+1)^3$ for $k \ge 4$.
Combining $3^{k+1} > 3k^3$ and $3k^3 > (k+1)^3$, we conclude $3^{k+1} > (k+1)^3$.
By the principle of mathematical induction, $3^n > n^3$ for all $n > 3$.

### Part (b)(i)
The sphere $S$ centered at the origin with radius $r=5$ has the equation:
$$x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 5^2 = 25$$
The line is given by $x = 3 - 7\lambda, y = 4 - \lambda, z = 0$.
Substitute the line equations into the sphere equation:
$$(3 - 7\lambda)^2 + (4 - \lambda)^2 + 0^2 = 25$$
$$(9 - 42\lambda + 49\lambda^2) + (16 - 8\lambda + \lambda^2) = 25$$
$$50\lambda^2 - 50\lambda + 25 = 25$$
$$50\lambda^2 - 50\lambda = 0$$
$$50\lambda(\lambda - 1) = 0$$
This gives $\lambda = 0$ or $\lambda = 1$.

If $\lambda = 0$, the point is $(3, 4, 0)$.
If $\lambda = 1$, the point is $(3-7, 4-1, 0) = (-4, 3, 0)$.
The points of intersection are $(3, 4, 0)$ and $(-4, 3, 0)$.

### Part (b)(ii)
The shortest distance $d$ from the origin $O(0,0,0)$ to the line $\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{a} + \lambda\mathbf{v}$ is given by:
$$d = \frac{|\vec{OA} \times \mathbf{v}|}{|\mathbf{v}|}$$
Where $A(3, 4, 0)$ is a point on the line and $\mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} -7 \\ -1 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}$.
$\vec{OA} \times \mathbf{v} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ 3 & 4 & 0 \\ -7 & -1 & 0 \end{vmatrix} = \mathbf{k}(3(-1) - 4(-7)) = \mathbf{k}(-3 + 28) = 25\mathbf{k}$.
The magnitude is $|25\mathbf{k}| = 25$.
The magnitude of the direction vector $|\mathbf{v}| = \sqrt{(-7)^2 + (-1)^2 + 0^2} = \sqrt{49 + 1} = \sqrt{50} = 5\sqrt{2}$.
$$d = \frac{25}{5\sqrt{2}} = \frac{5}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{5\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 3.54$$
The shortest distance is $\frac{5\sqrt{2}}{2}$ units.
