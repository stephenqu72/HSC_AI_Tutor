### Part a: Analysis of Inequalities

**1. The inequality $1 \leq |z - i| \leq 2$:**
This represents the region between and including two circles centered at $z_0 = i$ (which is the coordinate $(0, 1)$ in the Cartesian plane).
*   $|z - i| = 1$ is a circle with center $(0, 1)$ and radius $r_1 = 1$.
*   $|z - i| = 2$ is a circle with center $(0, 1)$ and radius $r_2 = 2$.
The inequality describes an **annulus** (a ring shape) centered at $(0, 1)$ with radii $1$ and $2$.

**2. The inequality $\text{Im}(z) \geq 0$:**
This corresponds to the upper half-plane, including the real axis ($y \geq 0$). We must restrict our annulus to this region.

### Calculation of Intercepts

*   **$y$-intercepts:** Set $x = 0$.
    *   Inner circle: $|iy - i| = 1 \implies |y-1| = 1 \implies y = 2$ or $y = 0$.
    *   Outer circle: $|iy - i| = 2 \implies |y-1| = 2 \implies y = 3$ or $y = -1$.
    Since $y \geq 0$, the $y$-intercepts are $(0, 0), (0, 2),$ and $(0, 3)$.

*   **$x$-intercepts:** Set $y = 0$. The equation is $|x + iy - i| = |x + i(y-1)| = \sqrt{x^2 + (y-1)^2}$.
    *   Inner circle: $\sqrt{x^2 + (-1)^2} = 1 \implies x^2 + 1 = 1 \implies x = 0$. (Already found).
    *   Outer circle: $\sqrt{x^2 + (-1)^2} = 2 \implies x^2 + 1 = 4 \implies x^2 = 3 \implies x = \pm\sqrt{3}$.
    The $x$-intercepts are $(-\sqrt{3}, 0)$ and $(\sqrt{3}, 0)$.
