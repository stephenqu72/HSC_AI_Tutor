### Rules of Negation
When negating a statement involving quantifiers:
1.  The universal quantifier $\forall$ becomes the existential quantifier $\exists$.
2.  The existential quantifier $\exists$ becomes the universal quantifier $\forall$.
3.  The predicate (the condition $xy = 1$) is negated ($xy \neq 1$).

Applying these rules systematically:
*   The initial part $\forall x \in \mathbb{R}^+$ becomes $\exists x \in \mathbb{R}^+$.
*   The middle part $\exists y \in \mathbb{R}^+$ becomes $\forall y \in \mathbb{R}^+$.
*   The final part $xy = 1$ becomes $xy \neq 1$.

$$\exists x \in \mathbb{R}^+ \text{ such that } \forall y \in \mathbb{R}^+, xy \neq 1$$
