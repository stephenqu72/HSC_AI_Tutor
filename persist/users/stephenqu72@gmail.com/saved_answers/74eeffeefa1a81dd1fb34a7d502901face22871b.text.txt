To solve the problems involving the lines $R_1$ and $R_2$, we represent them as sets of parametric equations.
The lines are given by:
$R_1: x = 4, \quad y = \lambda, \quad z = 1 + \lambda$
$R_2: x = 3 + \mu, \quad y = 3\mu, \quad z = 2 + 2\mu$

### Part i
To find the point of intersection, we set the components of $R_1$ equal to the components of $R_2$:
1) $4 = 3 + \mu \implies \mu = 1$
2) $\lambda = 3\mu$
3) $1 + \lambda = 2 + 2\mu$

Substitute $\mu = 1$ into equation (2):
$\lambda = 3(1) = 3$

Now, we must verify if these values satisfy equation (3) to confirm the lines intersect:
Left-hand side (LHS) of (3): $1 + \lambda = 1 + 3 = 4$
Right-hand side (RHS) of (3): $2 + 2\mu = 2 + 2(1) = 4$
Since LHS = RHS, the lines intersect at the point where $\lambda = 3$ (or $\mu = 1$).

Calculate the coordinates using $\lambda = 3$ in $R_1$:
$x = 4$

**Final Answer:** The lines intersect at $(4, 3, 4)$.

### Part ii
To find the shortest distance from point $P(1, 4, 0)$ to line $R_1$, we use the formula for the distance $d$ from a point $\mathbf{p}$ to a line $\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{a} + \lambda\mathbf{v}$:
$$d = \frac{|(\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{a}) \times \mathbf{v}|}{|\mathbf{v}|}$$
Here, $\mathbf{p} = (1, 4, 0)$, $\mathbf{a} = (4, 0, 1)$, and $\mathbf{v} = (0, 1, 1)$.

1. Calculate the vector $(\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{a})$:
$\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{a} = (1-4, 4-0, 0-1) = (-3, 4, -1)$

2. Compute the cross product $(\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{a}) \times \mathbf{v}$:
$$(-3, 4, -1) \times (0, 1, 1) = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ -3 & 4 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \end{vmatrix}$$
$= \mathbf{i}(4(1) - (-1)(1)) - \mathbf{j}(-3(1) - (-1)(0)) + \mathbf{k}(-3(1) - 4(0))$
$= \mathbf{i}(5) - \mathbf{j}(-3) + \mathbf{k}(-3) = (5, 3, -3)$

3. Calculate the magnitudes:
$|(\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{a}) \times \mathbf{v}| = \sqrt{5^2 + 3^2 + (-3)^2} = \sqrt{25 + 9 + 9} = \sqrt{43}$
$|\mathbf{v}| = \sqrt{0^2 + 1^2 + 1^2} = \sqrt{2}$

4. Calculate the distance $d$:
$d = \frac{\sqrt{43}}{\sqrt{2}} = \sqrt{\frac{43}{2}} = \frac{\sqrt{86}}{2}$

**Final Answer:** The shortest distance is $\frac{\sqrt{86}}{2}$ units.
